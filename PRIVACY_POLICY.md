## Overview

Plural Space is a private system management tool for plural individuals and systems. This policy describes how the app handles your data.

---

## Data Collection

**Plural Space collects no data from you.**

The app does not:
- Transmit any personal information to external servers
- Connect to the internet (except when you explicitly use the email export feature, which opens your device's native mail app)
- Use analytics, crash reporting services, or third-party SDKs that collect user data
- Require account registration or login
- Track usage, behavior, or location

---

## Data Storage

All data entered into Plural Space — including system name, member profiles, journal entries, and front history — is stored exclusively on your device using Android's AsyncStorage. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to export it using the in-app export tools.

---

## Export Features

Plural Space includes optional export features you control entirely:

- **JSON export:** Saves a file to your device's local storage and opens the Android share sheet. You decide where it goes.
- **HTML export:** Same as above. The file is for your personal use (e.g. uploading to Google Drive).
- **Email export:** Opens your device's native mail application with pre-filled content. Plural Space does not send email directly and has no access to your email account.

---

## Sensitive Information

Plural Space is designed for use by plural systems, which may include individuals with dissociative identity disorder (DID), other specified dissociative disorder (OSDD), or other plurality experiences. The app treats all entered information as private by default. No information about system identity, mental health, or personal history is collected or shared by the app.

---

## Children

Plural Space is not directed at children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect information from children.
